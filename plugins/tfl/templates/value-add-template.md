# Value-add template

The skeleton tfl-write-value-add fills. One format, five sections, always in this
order. The draft runs about **300 to 500 words across up to two pages** — short
enough that a founder reads it in a minute or two. When the member's offer runs
longer than 6 weeks, the sprint grows to 2 or 3 phases (see the note at the end);
nothing else changes. The section order never changes.

```
# [Company] - Fractional [Function] Snapshot

### What I can see from the outside
- 3 bullets, 50 words max each. Bold a 2-4 word lead-in that names what the
  observation is about (Your product, Who you serve, How you're growing), then
  one neutral-or-positive observation and what it probably implies for THIS
  company. Every bullet contains a checkable fact from the confirmed company
  profile. Sentiment test: the founder nods "yep" at all 3.

### Typical bottlenecks at this stage
(These are patterns I see at similar companies)
- Up to 3 bullets, one sentence each. Copied word for word from the company
  profile's Bottlenecks section. Stage-level phrasing only ("tends to," "usually,"
  "often"); never worded as a verdict about them.

### What a Fractional [Offer] Sprint could look like
**Week 1:** Understand. One line: the audit.
**Weeks 2-[N]:** Ship. One or two lines: fix or build one thing. [N] comes from
the member's sprint length in about/tfl-fractional-offer.md.

### If it's helpful
Exactly these 2 sentences, brackets filled in: "If any of this is even
directionally right, I'm happy to run it with your actual situation and see if a
fractional [title] engagement could make sense for [Company]. 

Worst case, you walk away with a clearer [function] scorecard you can run internally or hand to your next full-time hire."

- [Career win with a real number]
```

Caps recap: 3 observations, up to 3 bottlenecks, a 2-line sprint, 5 scorecard rows,
a 2-sentence close, 3 background bullets, 300 to 500 words total. If a section wants
to run longer, the asset is doing too much. Hold the line.

## When the offer runs longer than 6 weeks

A 2-line sprint undersells a long engagement: the founder needs to see the phases to
believe the path. When the sprint length in `about/tfl-fractional-offer.md` runs
longer than 6 weeks (a 90-day setup, a quarterly build), the sprint becomes 2 or 3
phases, one line each, with week ranges that sum to the offer length. The shape:
early weeks map and pick the highest-value targets, the middle builds the first
fixes, the final stretch tunes on real use and hands over a playbook or roadmap the
founder keeps either way.

Everything else holds: same five sections, same order, same 300-to-500-word budget,
same close, 5 scorecard rows, 3 background bullets. Wordiness kills replies faster
than missing detail does.
