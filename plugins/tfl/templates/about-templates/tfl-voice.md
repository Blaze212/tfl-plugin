## House rules (apply to every draft)

Write like a sharp human who happens to be typing.

- Short paragraphs, 1-2 sentences. Get to the point.
- Contractions always. Talk to one person, "I" and "you."
- Be specific: numbers, names, concrete details. Numbers beat adjectives.
- Hedge honestly when unsure: "likely," "tends to," "my read is."
- One idea per sentence. If you've made the point, stop.

## Banned (one of these appears, the draft fails)

- **Em dashes.** Use commas, colons, periods, or parentheses.
- **Negative-parallelism reframes:** "it's not X, it's Y," "forget X, do Y," "you don't need X, you need Y." Delete the negation, say the Y.
- **Dead AI words:** leverage, unlock, streamline, robust, seamless, navigate, elevate, empower, delve, landscape, game-changer, cutting-edge, holistic, synergy, deep dive, transformative, optimize.
- **Dead phrases:** "In today's...", "It's worth noting," "Let's dive in," "at the end of the day," "in order to."
- **Puffery:** "a pivotal moment," "marking a significant shift." State the fact, let the reader judge.
- **The rule of three:** "speed, efficiency, and innovation." Use 2 things, or 4, or the one that matters.
- **Hype:** fake urgency, big promises, guarantees, "10x your anything."
- **Engagement bait:** "Let that sink in," "read that again."
